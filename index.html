1

Goal Attraction

F_goal = k_att × normalize(goal − pos)

Pull the drone toward the target with constant magnitude k_att. This is the baseline steering force.

2

Relative Motion

relPos = obs_pos − drone_pos · relVel = obs_vel − drone_vel

Compute the relative position and velocity between drone and each obstacle within the detection range. Obstacles beyond the range are fully ignored.

3

Time to Closest Approach

t_ca = −dot(relPos, relVel) / |relVel|²

Find the future time (clamped to horizon T) when drone and obstacle will be at minimum separation. Project both positions to that time.

4

Urgency Assessment

urgency = 1 − closest_dist / safe_radius

If the closest approach distance is inside the safety envelope, urgency is 0→1. A time factor boosts urgency for imminent collisions.

5

Blended Avoidance Force

F_rep = (away×0.45 + perp×0.55) × k_rep × urgency

Steer away from the predicted collision point (45%) and perpendicular to the obstacle's path in the goal-friendly direction (55%). This actually moves out of the way.

6

Stall Escape

if stall_frames ≥ threshold → inject escape_vec

If the drone stops making progress, inject a randomised escape vector biased toward the goal direction to break out of local minima.

PCAN Algorithm Reference

PCAN — Predictive Cone-based Avoidance Navigation — is a hybrid reactive-predictive method combining:

  • Potential Field Navigation — attractive goal force + repulsive obstacle forces
  • Velocity Obstacle Methods — predicting future obstacle positions using current velocity
  • Random Restart Heuristics — escaping local minima when stalled

Key Differentiators from Classical PF

  1. Predictive: reacts to where obstacles will be, not where they are now
  2. Cone-filtered: only obstacles in the forward threat cone trigger repulsion
  3. Alignment-weighted: head-on threats get exponentially higher repulsion
  4. Stall-aware: deterministic escape from local minima

Viva Questions to Prepare

  • Why max(0, dot(...))? — Clamps diverging obstacles so they don't attract
  • Why 1 + alignment not just alignment? — Guarantees non-zero repulsion even for perpendicular movers
  • Why 30 frames? — Empirical threshold; too low = jitter, too high = stuck too long
  • Why 45° escape? — Biases toward goal rather than random direction; still breaks symmetry
